# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want PrepIQ to be a welcoming, respectful, and technically rigorous open-source community. Contributors, maintainers, mentors, and users are expected to collaborate in ways that keep the project useful and sustainable.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful in reviews, discussions, and issue threads.
- Focus criticism on code, product, and process rather than people.
- Be explicit about assumptions, blockers, and tradeoffs.
- Help new contributors understand context where practical.
- Respect maintainer time with reproducible reports and clear PRs.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or abusive language
- Personal attacks or intimidation
- Deliberate disruption of project discussions
- Sharing private information without permission
- Repeated bad-faith or spammy contributions

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers may edit, remove, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that violate this code of conduct or materially damage the collaboration environment.

## Enforcement

Examples of enforcement actions include:

- private warning
- temporary suspension from discussions
- closing or locking threads
- removal from contributor spaces
- permanent ban from the project

## Reporting

Report conduct issues privately to the maintainers with links, timestamps, and any context required to understand the concern.
